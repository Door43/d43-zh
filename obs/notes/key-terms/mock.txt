====== mock嘲笑/嘲讽/讥笑 ======

===== Definition: =====

定义：

To deride or make fun of someone, especially in a cruel way.

嘲笑或嘲讽某人，特别是用残忍(冷酷的）的方式。

  * Mocking often involves taunting someone and laughing at them.
  * 嘲笑通常包含了嘲讽/嘲弄某人或笑话他们。
  * To mock someone is to pretend to honor them, but in a way that actually shows contempt for them.
  * 嘲弄某人，就是假装表扬他们，但实际上是以鄙视他们的方式做的。
  * A person can also mock someone by imitating that person's words or actions with the intent to embarrass them.
  * 人们也可以通过模仿他人的话或动作，带有使人尴尬的意图，来嘲讽人。

===== Examples from the Bible stories: =====

  * **[[:zh:obs:notes:frames:21-12|[21-12]]]** Isaiah prophesied that people would spit on, **mock**, and beat the Messiah.
  * **[[:zh:obs:notes:frames:39-05|[39-05]]]** The Jewish leaders all answered the high priest, "He deserves to die!" Then they blindfolded Jesus, spit on him, hit him, and **mocked**  him.
  * **[[:zh:obs:notes:frames:39-12|[39-12]]]** The soldiers whipped Jesus, and put a royal robe and a crown made of thorns on him. Then they **mocked**  him by saying, "Look, the King of the Jews!"
  * **[[:zh:obs:notes:frames:40-04|[40-04]]]** Jesus was crucified between two robbers. One of them **mocked**  Jesus, but the other said, "Do you have no fear of God?"
  * **[[:zh:obs:notes:frames:40-05|[40-05]]]** The Jewish leaders and the other people in the crowd **mocked**  Jesus. They said to him, "If you are the Son of God, come down from the cross and save yourself! Then we will believe you."
